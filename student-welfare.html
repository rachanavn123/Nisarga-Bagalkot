Co-curricular Activities

Mentoring and Counselling

  • Mentoring is a one-to-one Student - Teacher interaction wherein a teacher keeps a complete update of the student.
  • As a student-centred academy, we are aware that personal problems can interfere with student's ability to study and be successful. Hence to take care of students overall development 10 students were monitored by one Mentor.
  • Issues that students may need support with include stress, life crises, depression, anxiety, self-esteem, confidence and goal setting. Counsellors work with students to help them find strategies and solutions that are best for them.
  • To motivate and improve the student’s confidence level regular personality development programmes are conducting.

Disciplinary Measures

  • The student must be punctual and regular in attending all classes.
  • Students are not allowed to leave the college premises during the college hours.
  • A Minimum attendance of 75% in each subject is a must to qualify for the Annual examination.
  • Cell phones are strictly prohibited in college campus.
  • Student found with cell phones if confiscated by the staff will not be returned.
  • Attendance is compulsory. Parents or Guardians should contact the Mentor within the first three days of absence.
  • The college stresses a strict code in term of dress, demeanour and discipline.
  • The tests / exams conducted by the college should be diligently attended by the students and should be considered as an intrinsic part of the learning process.
  • College property (be it benches, desk, books or lab equipment) will be treated with great care and consideration. Any damage or destruction shall be punishable.
  • Students shall maintain a sense of discipline at all times. Those guilty of misconduct or whose presence threatens the discipline of the college are liable to be expelled.
  • Late coming to college will be viewed seriously.
